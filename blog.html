Update 11/24/25

Happy (early) Thanksgiving!

I had a great idea for a Thanksgiving-themed crossword last week, so I started working on it. Then, midway through making it, I had ANOTHER idea for a Thanksgiving-themed crossword. My original plan was to finish making and playtesting both puzzles and then pick one of them to upload this week, but because I think they both turned out so good, I'm uploading BOTH! It's a Thanksgiving miracle!

The first crossword is a Tuesday-difficulty puzzle that, despite being a simple theme, was a tricky one to make due to the interesting seed words. Aside from a couple difficult clues, this one shouldn't be too bad. The second crossword is a Wednesday-difficulty puzzle that revolves around a very specific episode of a very specific television show. None of my playtesters knew the reference, but I'm sure my parents and a few people from an older demographic will understand it (notice I didn't say "old", I said "older"!). I gave it a Wednesday-difficulty tag because of its relative obscurity, but even if you don't know the show, you'll still be able to figure it out. Extra note about that one, I believe this is my first puzzle on the site that uses left-right mirror symmetry instead of the classic rotational symmetry. I always try to do rotational symmetry if I can, but this puzzle just worked so much better without it.

As always, I am also uploading 3 more weeklies to the site! I've noticed that the Nibble on average tends to take people longer to solve than the Bite. This was not my intention, but I think this is due to the fill on the Nibble being a bit more difficult for me than the Bite just because of the small size. I'll be keeping this in mind more in the future because I do want to have a consistent progression of difficulty with the puzzles. This week, I also wanted to challenge myself more with the Bit by doing a 5x5 grid with zero blocks. That means the grid contains only 5-letter entries. A couple entries are definitely harder than what I would normally accept for a Bit, but I think the clues should do most of the heavy lifting here.

Anyways, I think that's enough yapping out of me. Happy solving everyone!

- Buck

Update 6/5/24

Time for another batch of puzzles! I'm releasing my newest puzzle, "Big Cheese", along with nine of my older puzzles. You may notice that a lot of the puzzles I'm releasing today are marked as Tuesday difficulty. Most of them I was tempted to mark as Monday puzzles, but there were often too many difficult words or clues included for me to feel comfortable marking them as such. Nevertheless, I think this new batch has some really fun themes I'm quite proud of (especially "Breakfast Cereal" and "Nature's Gift").

I did make one addition to the website that some people may find exciting. I was recently asked if my puzzles were playable offline, to which I explained that solving my puzzles through the website does require an internet connection. However, if you didn't realize, I had included a link at the bottom of each page that allows you to download the .puz file, which you can open through any application that allows you to open .puz files (I recently found the app "Puzzazz" which lets you open .puz files through it which is pretty helpful). Though, I decided I'd make an even easier option for those who may not be as tech savvy as me and allow you to download a PDF version of all of my crosswords. So now you should be able to solve my puzzles online, offline, and right off the printer if you enjoy the classic pencil and paper approach.

Hope you're all doing well so far this summer and I hope you all enjoy the crosswords! Happy solving!

- Buck

Update 5/24/24

Boy, did I give this site an overhaul or what? Figured it'd be fun to do an occasional blog post here to give updates on when I release new puzzles or make changes to the website. When I first created this website, I only uploaded my latest 10 puzzles. This is because I wanted to test the waters and see how viable putting all my puzzles here would be, and so far, it seems to be working out great!

Today, I just released a new puzzle I made this month along with 9 others I made in the last few months of 2023. I still have a ton of older puzzles I need to put on this website, but I think I'll do it in smaller chunks as to make the process less tedious. I also want to make changes to some of my older puzzles as well. Some of them don't meet my standards for what I think is a good puzzle today.

I'll definitely be changing website hosts in the near future so I'll be sure to update you all when that happens. I'd love any feedback you have, whether that be for a specific puzzle or for the website as a whole. You can send me a message through Discord or through my Gmail account. Happy solving!
